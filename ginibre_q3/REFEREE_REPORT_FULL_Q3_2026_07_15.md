## Scope ruling

The manuscript proves a full-Q3 theorem for the closed multiplicative convex
cone generated by the real adjoint character.  Here “full” legitimately means
all finite tuple lengths, all sign assignments, every even number of minus
factors, and Ginibre's Proposition 1 promotion from the generator to that
cone.

It cannot be presented as a nonabelian extension of the full cone in Ginibre's
1970 Example 4.  Example 4 uses **all real continuous positive-definite
functions**.  That literal nonabelian statement is false already on `SO(3)`.

I checked this scope directly against the archived primary paper.  Ginibre's
Section 1, condition (Q3), equation (1.2), quantifies over every finite family
in the chosen cone and every assignment of plus or minus signs.  Proposition 1
and equation (1.5) promote a self-conjugate generating set to its norm-closed
multiplicative convex cone.  Section 2, Example 4, takes that cone to be all
real positive-definite functions for the stated compact abelian groups, while
the conclusion explicitly proposes the same cone on a noncommutative compact
group, including the rotation group, as an open candidate.  Thus the
manuscript reproduces the original finite-family/sign/cone-closure structure,
and its `SO(3)` computation answers Ginibre's proposed literal candidate in the
negative rather than silently weakening it.

For a unit vector `v`, set

```text
f_v(g) = <v,gv>.
```

Each `f_v` is real, continuous, and positive definite.  Take

```text
v1 = (sqrt(19/31),  sqrt(12/31), 0),
v2 = (sqrt(19/31), -sqrt(12/31), 0),
v3 = v4 = (1,0,0).
```

Haar projection gives

```text
E[f_u f_v] = <u,v>^2/3
```

and, with the three pairing contractions `c1,c2,c3`,

```text
E[f_1 f_2 f_3 f_4]
  = (2/15) sum_i c_i^2 - (1/15) sum_{i<j} c_i c_j.
```

Here `(c1,c2,c3)=(7,19,19)/31`, so the fourth moment is `61/961` and the
Q3 signed product with signs `--++` is exactly

```text
2[61/961 + (49-361-361)/(9*961)] = -8/279.
```

Thus no missing estimate or larger computation can produce a literal
full-cone match.  The paper correctly retains this counterexample and limits
its affirmative theorem to the adjoint-generated cone.  It makes no claim
about the separate cone of all real central positive-definite functions.
The direct page-level record is
`referee_audits/GINIBRE_1970_SCOPE_SOURCE_AUDIT.md`; the archived primary PDF
has SHA-256 `8c6b3d4e...a4cf07`.

## Related-work and novelty ruling

The original report's scope ruling was mathematically correct but its
publication assessment was incomplete without two directly adjacent sources.
That gap is now closed in the manuscript and in
`references/GINIBRE_RELATED_WORK_SOURCE_AUDIT.md`.

Sylvester, *The Ginibre inequality*, Comm. Math. Phys. 73 (1980), 105--114,
already constructed graph-cycle counterexamples for higher-dimensional
`O(N)` spins.  Accordingly, the paper must not claim the first nonabelian
counterexample to a Ginibre inequality.  Its negative contribution is more
specific: a different exact witness directly in the compact-group cone
proposed in Ginibre's conclusion, using four explicit positive-definite
functions on `SO(3)` and giving `-8/279` under normalized Haar integration.

Abdesselam, arXiv:2207.07603 (2022), formulates general and padded Ginibre
inequalities for invariant `O(N)` and projective-spin observables.  His
Theorems 2.1--2.2 are large-power asymptotics and Theorem 2.3 proves the padded
inequalities for inverse half-integer powers of stable determinantal
polynomials.  These are important adjacent results, but they are not a proof
of the present fixed finite-rank compact-group Haar hierarchy.  Conversely,
the present adjoint-generated theorem does not settle finite-`N`
invariant-observable GKS2, which Abdesselam records as open for `N>=3`.

With this correction, the publishable novelty is accurately delimited as the
combination of (i) the exact compact-group `SO(3)` all-positive-definite
obstruction and (ii) the full finite-tuple/sign Q3 theorem on the
adjoint-generated cone for every compact connected simple type.

The revised manuscript also frames quantum applications at the correct
strength.  The theorem may be an algebraic input for group-valued lattice
models whose interactions lie in the adjoint-generated cone.  It is not, by
itself, a correlation theorem for quantum Heisenberg or general `O(N)` vector
spins, nonlinear sigma models, lattice gauge theories, or continuum QFT.
Adjoint representation content alone does not identify gauge plaquette
interactions with this cone or resolve shared-link constraints.  Any quantum
application still requires model-specific Gibbs promotion, thermodynamic
limits, and reflection-positive transfer-matrix or equivalent reconstruction.
This limitation materially narrows the applications but does not weaken the
stated harmonic-analysis theorem.

The publication revision now cites Chevyrev--Garban's 2025 Villain-action
work at the exact loci separating its nonabelian lattice-gauge limit from its
abelian Ginibre monotonicity corollary.  It also identifies the author,
resolves Parts I--II to the same tagged source archive as Part III, and adds a

## Mathematical assessment

The reduction

```text
I^G_{a,n} = E[(X-Y)^(2a) (X+Y)^n] >= 0,  a,n >= 0,
X = chi_ad(g), Y = chi_ad(h),
```

correctly captures all even-minus Q3 inequalities for the singleton adjoint
generator.  Odd-minus sectors vanish under interchange of the two Haar
variables; even `n` is pointwise nonnegative; and the all-plus sector is a sum
of nonnegative invariant multiplicities.  The exactly-two-minus theorem is
stated as a numbered imported theorem and formally tied to the cited companion
Parts I--II.  Ginibre's factorization then promotes the singleton result to
positive sums, products, and uniform limits in the adjoint-generated cone.

For the remaining classical hierarchy, the paper now derives the required
bounded-Littlewood moment formulas from the determinant identities of
Huh--Kim--Krattenthaler--Okada, including the even-orthogonal component.  Its
two-power coefficient functional reduces those identities to finite exact
integer arithmetic.  Stable prefixes, analytic tails, and the numbered
residual-certificate proposition cover the previously open ranks
`B_2..B_17`, `C_2..C_28`, and `D_4..D_30`.  The all-classical theorem and final
all-types theorem therefore have no remaining finite box or tail hypothesis.

The type-A, exceptional, and remaining classical-rank branches retain their
separate proved prefix/tail overlaps.  Together these branches cover every
compact connected group with simple Lie algebra, for the stated cone.
There is no low-rank classification omission: `B_1` and `C_1` are type
`A_1`, `D_3` is type `A_3`, while `D_2` is not simple; the stated classical
ranges therefore begin exactly at `B_2`, `C_2`, and `D_4`.  A common compact
simply connected cover and Lemma on central-cover invariance then give every
compact connected global form.

I directly checked the load-bearing bounded-Littlewood citation against page
3 of the archived Huh--Kim--Krattenthaler--Okada primary PDF, rather than
against the manuscript's transcription.  At `w=b` and `u=0`, Theorem 1.3,
equations (1.9)--(1.10), gives the sum and difference of the zero-odd-column
and `2b+1`-odd-column sectors.  Their half-sum is exactly the paper's type-B
formula, including the alternating elementary factor and the signs
`f_{i-j}-f_{i+j-1}` and `f_{i-j}+f_{i+j-1}`.  Equation (1.6), after the Hall
involution, gives the type-C determinant with shift `i+j`; equation (1.7) at
`u=0` gives the two type-D sectors with the source's existing factor `1/2`
and shift `i+j-2`.  Thus no determinant sign, width wall, index shift, or
factor of two is inferred from the code.  The detailed source audit is
`referee_audits/HKKO_BOUNDED_LITTLEWOOD_SOURCE_AUDIT.md`; the primary PDF
SHA-256 is `ae94e04a...dd8bb2`.

I likewise direct-read the paragraph preceding Lemma 3.1 in Rains' 1998
primary paper.  It states that the `O(N)` integral of `s_lambda` selects
exactly even partitions of length at most `N`, while the `Sp(2b)` integral
selects exactly partitions of length at most `2b` whose part multiplicities
are even, equivalently whose transpose is even.  These are exactly the B/C
criteria used before the determinant identities.  For `SO(2b)`, adding the
determinant-twisted `O(2b)` average to the untwisted average selects the
identity component; twisting adds one full column, so Rains' even-part
criterion becomes the paper's sector of exactly `2b` odd rows.  Removing that
column yields `lambda=(1+2nu_1,...,1+2nu_(2b))` and `|nu|=j-b`, with no extra
normalization.  The detailed check is
`referee_audits/RAINS_CLASSICAL_SCHUR_SOURCE_AUDIT.md`; the archived PDF
SHA-256 is `d5242a4d...80cdc3`.
