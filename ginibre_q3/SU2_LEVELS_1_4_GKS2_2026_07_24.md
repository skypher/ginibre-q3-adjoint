# Uniform `GKS2*` for the fusion rings `SU(2)_k`, `1<=k<=4`

Date: 2026-07-24

## Status

This note proves Ginibre's signed-product condition `GKS2*` for **arbitrary
word length** in each of the four fusion rings

```text
SU(2)_1, SU(2)_2, SU(2)_3, SU(2)_4.
```

The levels one through three have closed spectral-table proofs.  Level four
has an exact finite all-exponent certificate: 258 of 272 support/parity
regimes are proved by coordinatewise capacitated transport, and the remaining
fourteen reduce to two elementary inequalities.

Because an even-minus word is invariant under exchanging its two tensor
factors, the same theorem implies the full partial-character column at each
of these levels.

## 1. Setup and two reductions

Let `R_k` be the `SU(2)_k` fusion ring with simple basis
`V_0,...,V_k`.  For labels `a_i` and signs `epsilon_i in {+1,-1}` put

```text
W=product_i (V_(a_i) tensor V_0
             +epsilon_i V_0 tensor V_(a_i)).          (1.1)
```

`GKS2*` is the assertion

```text
[V_0 tensor V_0]W >= 0                               (1.2)
```

when the number of minus factors is even.  Odd minus parity gives zero by
exchange antisymmetry.  All-plus words are nonnegative because all fusion
coefficients are nonnegative.

If one label occurs with both signs, pair one occurrence of each sign:

```text
(V_a tensor 1-1 tensor V_a)(V_a tensor 1+1 tensor V_a)
 =V_a^2 tensor 1-1 tensor V_a^2.                      (1.3)
```

Since `V_a^2` is a nonnegative sum of simples, (1.3) writes the coefficient
as a nonnegative sum of coefficients with one fewer factor.  Induction
reduces the proof to **support-disjoint chambers**: every nontrivial simple
label is absent, occurs only with plus sign, or occurs only with minus sign.

For the spectral calculations below, the standard Verlinde diagonalization is
used in the following normalization.  At node `j=0,...,k`,

```text
w_j=2/(k+2) sin^2((j+1)pi/(k+2)),
chi_a(j)=sin((a+1)(j+1)pi/(k+2))
         /sin((j+1)pi/(k+2)).                         (1.4)
```

The vacuum functional is `tau(f)=sum_j w_j f(j)`.  Consequently the corner
of (1.1) is

```text
sum_(i,j) w_i w_j product_a
  (chi_a(i)+epsilon_a chi_a(j))^(p_a).                 (1.5)
```

If a minus label is active, every diagonal term `i=j` vanishes.

Finally, once (1.2) is known for every word, let `W` have even minus parity.
Exchange symmetry gives

```text
[V_0 tensor V_0](V_c tensor 1+1 tensor V_c)W
 =2[V_c tensor V_0]W.                                (1.6)
```

The left side is another `GKS2*` corner.  Hence every partial-character
coefficient `[V_c tensor V_0]W` is nonnegative.

## 2. Level one

The two spectral nodes have weight `1/2`, and the only nontrivial character
has values `1,-1`.  In a support-disjoint chamber containing a minus factor,
all factors have minus sign and their exponent is even.  The sole off-diagonal
pair contributes a positive even power of `2`.  Thus `SU(2)_1` satisfies
full `GKS2*` and, by (1.6), full partial-character positivity.

## 3. Level two

Write `A=V_1`, `J=V_2`.  The spectral values and weights are

```text
node             0          1          2
weight          1/4        1/2        1/4
A             sqrt(2)       0       -sqrt(2)
J                1         -1          1.             (3.1)
```

For a word containing a minus factor, diagonal spectral pairs vanish.  Divide
the remaining corner by the positive constant `2(1/4)(1/2)`.  The three
unordered node pairs have normalized weights `1,1/2,1`, and their bases are

```text
pair       A+       A-       J+       J-
01       sqrt(2)  sqrt(2)      0        2
02          0     2sqrt(2)     2        0
12      -sqrt(2)  sqrt(2)      0       -2.            (3.2)
```

Let `a,b` be the positive exponents of `A,J` when active.  After imposing
even total minus parity, every support-disjoint chamber is one of

```text
minus support    plus support       normalized corner
{J}              empty              2^b+(-2)^b
{J}              {A}                (sqrt(2))^a 2^b[1+(-1)^a]
{A}              empty              2(sqrt(2))^a+(1/2)(2sqrt(2))^a
{A}              {J}                (1/2)(2sqrt(2))^a 2^b
{A,J}            empty              (sqrt(2))^a 2^b[1+(-1)^b].      (3.3)
```

Here `b` is even in the first two rows, `a` is even in the next two, and
`a+b` is even in the last.  Every expression in (3.3) is nonnegative.
Therefore `SU(2)_2` satisfies full `GKS2*` and full partial-character
positivity.

## 4. Level three

Put

```text
A=V_1,       B=V_2,       J=V_3,
q=sqrt(5),   phi=(1+q)/2,
alpha=(5-q)/20,            beta=(5+q)/20=phi^2 alpha.
```

The spectral table is

```text
node       weight          A          B          J
0          alpha          phi        phi         1
1          beta           1/phi     -1/phi      -1
2          beta          -1/phi     -1/phi       1
3          alpha         -phi        phi        -1.    (4.1)
```

For a word with a minus factor, divide the off-diagonal spectral sum by the
positive constant `2 alpha beta`.  The complete pair table is

```text
pair  weight   A+    A-    B+    B-    J+    J-
01      1       q     1     1     q     0     2
02      1       1     q     1     q     2     0
03    phi^-2    0    q+1   q+1    0     0     2
12    phi^2     0    q-1   1-q    0     0    -2
13      1      -1     q     1    -q    -2     0
23      1      -q     1     1    -q     0     2.       (4.2)
```

Define, for `n>=1`,

```text
F_n=2+phi^(-2)(2phi)^n+(-1)^n phi^2(2/phi)^n.          (4.3)
```

Then `F_n>=0`: it is manifest for even `n`; `F_1=0`; and for odd `n>=3`,

```text
F_n=2+2^n(phi^(n-2)-phi^(2-n))>0.                     (4.4)
```

Let `a,b,r` denote the exponents of `A,B,J`.  A blank exponent corresponds
to an absent label.  Direct multiplication of (4.2) gives the following
complete support-disjoint chamber table.  Every row is understood with even
total minus parity.

```text
minus support  plus support       normalized corner
{J}            empty              5*2^r
{J}            {A}                2^r q^a[1+(-1)^a]
{J}            {B}                2^r F_b
{J}            {A,B}              2^r q^a[1+(-1)^a]

{B}            empty              4q^b
{B}            {A}                q^b[1+(-1)^a](1+q^a)
{B}            {J}                q^b2^r[1+(-1)^r]
{B}            {A,J}              q^b2^r[1+(-1)^(a+r)]

{A}            empty              2q^a+F_a
{A}            {B}                2q^a+F_(a+b)
{A}            {J}                q^a2^r[1+(-1)^r]
{A}            {B,J}              q^a2^r[1+(-1)^r]

{A,B}          empty              q^b[1+(-1)^b](1+q^a)
{A,B}          {J}                q^(a+b)2^r[1+(-1)^(b+r)]

{A,J}          empty              2^r F_a
{A,J}          {B}                2^r F_(a+b)

{B,J}          empty              q^b2^r[1+(-1)^b]
{B,J}          {A}                q^(a+b)2^r[1+(-1)^(a+b)]

{A,B,J}        empty              q^b2^r[1+(-1)^b].                 (4.5)
```

All factors outside `F_n` are positive or of the form `1+(-1)^m`, while
(4.3)--(4.4) prove `F_n>=0`.  Thus every row is nonnegative, proving full
`GKS2*` and full partial-character positivity for `SU(2)_3`.

## 5. A finite exponential-transport lemma

The level-four proof uses the following elementary form of capacitated
transport.

**Lemma 5.1.**  Let `r=(r_1,...,r_s)` range over nonnegative integer vectors
and write

```text
H(r)=sum_(p in P) c_p product_t lambda_(p,t)^r_t
     -sum_(q in N) d_q product_t mu_(q,t)^r_t,          (5.1)
```

with nonnegative bases and positive coefficients.  Join `q` to `p` when
`lambda_(p,t)>=mu_(q,t)` for every `t`.  If every subset `X` of `N` obeys

```text
sum_(q in X)d_q <= sum_(p adjacent to X)c_p,            (5.2)
```

then `H(r)>=0` for every `r`.

**Proof.**  The capacitated Hall theorem produces a nonnegative transport
from every negative coefficient to dominating positive coefficients, with
row sums `d_q` and column sums at most `c_p`.  Along every transport edge the
positive monomial dominates the negative monomial coordinatewise.  Summing
the transported inequalities and then the unused positive capacity proves
(5.1).  QED.

## 6. Level four spectral reduction

Write

```text
A=V_1,       B=V_2,       C=V_3,       J=V_4.
```

The five Verlinde nodes have the exact table

```text
node  weight       A       B       C       J
0      1/12      sqrt(3)   2     sqrt(3)   1
1      1/4          1      0       -1     -1
2      1/3          0     -1        0      1
3      1/4         -1      0        1     -1
4      1/12     -sqrt(3)   2    -sqrt(3)   1.          (6.1)
```

Fix a support-disjoint sign chamber and the parity of each positive exponent.
Write

```text
p_a=p_a^0+2r_a,       p_a^0 in {1,2},       r_a>=0.    (6.2)
```

For each unordered pair of distinct nodes `i<j`, its contribution is

```text
c_(ij) product_a lambda_((ij),a)^r_a,

c_(ij)=2w_iw_j product_a
       (chi_a(i)+epsilon_a chi_a(j))^(p_a^0),

lambda_((ij),a)
       =(chi_a(i)+epsilon_a chi_a(j))^2.               (6.3)
```

The sign of `c_(ij)` is fixed in a parity chamber and every residual base is
nonnegative.  Thus Lemma 5.1 applies after exact comparison in
`Q(sqrt(3))`.

The deterministic verifier `verify_su2_level4_transport.py` enumerates all
272 support/parity regimes.  It checks every Hall inequality over every
subset of negative spectral pairs using exact rational arithmetic in
`Q(sqrt(3))`.  Direct transport proves 258 regimes.

## 7. The fourteen level-four exceptions

The fourteen regimes not covered by coordinatewise transport use only two
extra inequalities.  Put

```text
L=4+2sqrt(3),       S=4-2sqrt(3),       LS=4.          (7.1)
```

The **radial inequality** is

```text
2*3^n+12^n >= 3*4^n,                    n>=0.          (R)
```

It is equality at `n=0`; for `n>=1`, the single term
`12^n>=3*4^n` already proves it.

The **crossed inequality** is

```text
L^u S^v+S^u L^v >= 2,                   u,v>=0.        (C)
```

Indeed AM-GM and `LS=4` give a lower bound
`2(LS)^((u+v)/2)=2*2^(u+v)>=2`.

In the following table the status string is in the order `A,B,C,J`; `0`
means absent, `+/-` is the sign, and the parity string uses `e,o,*` for even,
odd, inactive exponent.

```text
status   parity alternatives       completion
00-+     **eo                      radial
+0+-     o*oe                      crossed
+++-     eoee, oeoe               crossed
+-+-     eoeo, oeoe               crossed
-00+     e**o                      radial
-0-0     o*o*                      radial + crossed
-0-+     o*oe                      radial
-0--     o*oe                      crossed
-+--     eoee, oeoe               crossed
----     eoeo, oeoe               crossed.             (7.2)
```

Counting alternatives gives three radial regimes, ten crossed regimes, and
one combined regime.

For every crossed regime, the four mixed-node positive terms reduce, after
common positive factors are removed, to a positive multiple of the left side
of (C); the two remaining negative terms reduce to the corresponding
multiple of `2`.  Any active `B` residual exponent multiplies the positive
terms by `4^r` and the negative terms by `1`, so it only strengthens the
inequality.

For each radial regime, the three positive radial terms have residual bases
`3,12,3`, while the negative radial term has base `4`; their exact
coefficients reduce to (R).  The combined `-0-0` regime splits exactly into
one crossed comparison and one radial comparison.  Hence all fourteen
exceptions are nonnegative.

Together with the 258 exact Hall regimes, this proves `GKS2*` for arbitrary
word length in `SU(2)_4`.  Equation (1.6) proves the full partial-character
column.

## 8. Theorem and stable consequence

**Theorem 8.1.**  Every fusion ring `SU(2)_k` with `1<=k<=4` satisfies full
`GKS2*` for signed words of arbitrary length.  Every corresponding
partial-character coefficient is nonnegative.

Combining this with the sharp half-total stability theorem gives an ordinary
`SU(2)` corollary.  Let a signed word have labels `a_i`, total `T`, and even
minus parity.  If

```text
max(max_i a_i,ceil(T/2)) <= 4,                         (8.1)
```

then its ordinary Ginibre corner equals its corner in one of the proved
finite rings and is nonnegative.  More generally, the ordinary partial
coefficient at `V_c` is nonnegative whenever

```text
max(max_i a_i,c,ceil((T+c)/2)) <= 4.                   (8.2)
```

These finite-level theorems do not yet settle all levels; they provide the
first complete initial interval of fusion rings and a verified transport
model for attacking level five and above.

## 9. Reproducibility

Two independent verifiers accompany the proof.

1. `character_ring_iter/verify_su2_level4_transport.py` is the exact
   all-exponent certificate for the 272 level-four regimes.
2. `character_ring_iter/verify_su2_levels_1_4_regression.py` performs direct
   fusion-ring dynamic programming for every support-disjoint chamber with
   bounded exponents and checks every partial-character target.  This second
   program is a regression, not the all-exponent proof.

Recorded outputs and source hashes are in
`certificates/su2_levels_1_4_gks2.log`.
