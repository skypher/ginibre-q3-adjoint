By the odd-level simple-current lifting theorem, the complete fusion ring `SU(2)_9` therefore satisfies scalar `GKS2*`; adjoining one plus factor proves every partial-character coefficient is nonnegative.

## 5. Theorem

**Theorem 5.1.** The rank-five orbit ring `O_9` satisfies scalar `GKS2*` for signed words of arbitrary length.

**Proof.** Support overlap reduces to the 272 support/sign/parity chambers above. The residual-orthant decomposition is exhaustive. Pointwise positivity covers 560 regimes, and the exact certificate covers every one of the remaining 2,408 regimes by the five proved certificate classes. Hence every support-disjoint even-minus word has nonnegative scalar corner. Reversing the overlap reduction proves the theorem for all words. QED.

**Corollary 5.2.** The complete fusion ring `SU(2)_9` satisfies scalar `GKS2*` for every signed word of arbitrary length. Every partial-character coefficient is nonnegative.

## 6. Stable ordinary `SU(2)` consequence

For an ordinary signed word with total label sum `T` and target `V_c`, put

```text
K(c)=max(max_i a_i,c,ceil((T+c)/2)).
```

The finite theorems now prove every ordinary partial coefficient with

```text
K(c)<=9.
```

For the Ginibre corner, the sufficient condition is

```text
max(max_i a_i,ceil(T/2))<=9.
```

Thus every ordinary word with labels at most nine and total label sum at most eighteen is covered, together with the additional words allowed by the sharper combined bound.
