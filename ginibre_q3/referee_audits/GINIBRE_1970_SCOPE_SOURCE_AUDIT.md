# Direct source audit of Ginibre's 1970 Q3 scope

Primary source: J. Ginibre, *General formulation of Griffiths' inequalities*,
Comm. Math. Phys. 16 (1970), 310--328.  The archived PDF
`references/ginibre1970.pdf` has SHA-256
`8c6b3d4e99b3c22267ec9c46ef8fa44fd29be06e92ac7b3c6cb0b3ba5ba4cf07`.

The following points were checked directly in the primary PDF.

1. On journal page 311, condition Q3, equation (1.2), quantifies over every
   finite family `f_1,...,f_n` in the chosen cone and every sequence of plus
   or minus signs.  The next page explicitly notes that an odd number of
   minus signs makes the integral zero.
2. On journal pages 312--313, Ginibre defines `Q(S)` as the norm-closed
   multiplicative convex cone generated by a self-conjugate set `S`:
   equivalently, the norm closure of polynomials in `S` and `1` with
   nonnegative coefficients.  Proposition 1 says that Q3 for `S` implies Q3
   for `Q(S)`.  Equation (1.5) is the product-factorization step used in the
   induction.
3. On journal page 316, Example 4 takes an abelian compact group built from
   finitely many circle and finite-cyclic factors, normalized Haar measure,
   and `Q(S)` equal to the cone of all real positive-definite functions.
4. On journal page 328, the conclusion proposes the cone of real
   positive-definite functions on a noncommutative compact group, explicitly
   including the rotation group, as a candidate for further work.  It is not
   stated there as a proved theorem.

Therefore the present paper matches the original finite-family/sign
quantifiers and Proposition 1 promotion exactly when it proves Q3 for the
singleton generator `S={chi_ad}` and promotes to its norm-closed
multiplicative convex cone.  It does not match Example 4's larger cone of all
real positive-definite functions.  The paper's exact `SO(3)` value `-8/279`
shows that Ginibre's proposed literal noncommutative candidate is false, so
the adjoint-generated scope cannot honestly be enlarged to obtain that
different theorem.
