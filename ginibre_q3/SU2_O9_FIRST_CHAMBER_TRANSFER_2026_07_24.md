# Exact transfer formulation of the first unresolved `O_9` chamber

Date: 2026-07-24

## 1. Chamber

Use the rank-five orbit basis `B_0,...,B_4` for `O_9`. The first chamber not
covered by the separated-pole theorem and the existing pairwise transport
fan has

```text
B_1 minus, exponent 2+2p,
B_2 plus,  exponent 1,
B_4 minus, exponent 2+2q,            p,q>=0.           (1.1)
```

Let `N_a` be the integral fusion matrix for multiplication by `B_a`. Its
entries are determined by

```text
B_a B_b=sum_(c=|a-b|)^min(a+b,9-a-b) B_c.             (1.2)
```

On `O_9 tensor O_9`, define

```text
D_a=N_a tensor I-I tensor N_a,
S_a=N_a tensor I+I tensor N_a.                         (1.3)
```

If `e_00` is the tensor vacuum vector, the chamber corner is exactly

```text
F_(p,q)=e_00^T D_1^(2+2p) S_2 D_4^(2+2q)e_00.         (1.4)
```

## 2. Commuting two-parameter transfer

Put

```text
A=D_1^2,
C=D_4^2,
b=D_1^2 S_2 D_4^2 e_00.                              (2.1)
```

All fusion matrices commute, so all left-right signed tensor operators
commute. Therefore

```text
AC=CA,
F_(p,q)=e_00^T A^p C^q b.                             (2.2)
```

Everything in (2.2) is integral. The state space has dimension 25, so
Cayley--Hamilton gives exact constant-coefficient recurrences of order at
most 25 separately in `p` and in `q`. More sharply, the relevant recurrence
orders are the degrees of the minimal polynomials of `A` and `C` on the
cyclic subspace generated by `b` and observed by `e_00`.

Thus the all-index chamber problem is equivalent to proving

```text
F_(p,q)>=0                 for all p,q>=0              (2.3)
```

for one explicitly generated bivariate linear-recurrence array.

## 3. Why this is useful

The spectral transport failure involved algebraic weights and ten unordered
spectral pairs. Formula (2.2) removes all floating-point and algebraic-number
comparisons:

- every state and transition is an integer;
- rectangular values can be produced with two repeated matrix-vector
  multiplications;
- exact annihilating recurrences follow mechanically from the two transfer
  matrices;
- a proof may proceed through an invariant cone, a recurrence induction, or
  a rational bivariate generating function.

The dependency-free program

```text
character_ring_iter/analyze_su2_o9_first_chamber.py
```

constructs the fusion matrices from (1.2), verifies `AC=CA`, and evaluates
(2.2) over any requested rectangle using arbitrary-precision Python
integers. It deliberately labels its rectangular PASS output as diagnostic:
a bounded nonnegative table is not the all-index proof of (2.3).

## 4. Next exact certificate

A complete certificate for this chamber can now have one of three compact
forms.

1. **Positive recurrence:** exact recurrences in `p,q` together with a finite
   induction region whose recurrence coefficients preserve a suitable cone.
2. **Rational generating function:** an exact expression

   ```text
   G(x,y)=sum_(p,q>=0)F_(p,q)x^p y^q
          =e_00^T(I-xA)^(-1)(I-yC)^(-1)b,              (4.1)
   ```

   followed by a coefficient-positive decomposition.
3. **Finite invariant cone:** finitely many integral linear forms containing
   `b`, preserved by both `A` and `C`, with `e_00` nonnegative on the cone.

The transfer formulation makes these alternatives exhaustive finite
algebra problems. It also provides a reusable template for every residual
orthant of every rank-five orbit chamber.
