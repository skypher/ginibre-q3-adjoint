# Certificate cost audit

This note separates theorem obligations from historical overlaps and records
which expensive computations can be reduced without changing a theorem
statement. Runtime figures are observations from the authenticated replay
notes unless explicitly described as a current-source test.

## Changes already made

1. The Parts I--II residual propagation consumes correction offsets `0..27`.
   The former active contract also required offset `28`, while the replay ran
   offset `29` as a further overlap. The contract is now exactly 56 results
   (two families times 28 offsets), and H28--H29 are no longer mandatory
   clean-room stages. They remain authenticated historical data.
2. The theorem uses only the local half-stable bridge. It now imports the
   separately numbered local proposition rather than the stronger global
   bridge whose proof pulled surplus correction offsets onto the dependency
   graph.
3. The Part III boxes formerly split into 12,993 bounded-Littlewood cases and
   4,869 reverse-Pieri B/D cases use the same bounded-Littlewood determinant
   identity. The exact determinant ledger now covers all 17,862 cases through
   moment 71 in 114 rows. It uses 31 primes and a 961-bit CRT modulus, checks
   the old 4,869-case minimum exactly, and passed a strict current-source run
   on 64 workers in under one minute. The old reverse-Pieri suppliers are now
   optional independent controls.
4. The 5,509-case modular checker is also optional. It is a valuable
   implementation-independent audit of the original 70-row subledger, but it
   is not a second premise after the exact CRT reconstruction has exceeded the
   character bound.
5. The directed-MPFR verifier is explicitly frozen to its proved 58-row
   subledger (`B<=17`, `C<=28`, `D<=30`).  The additional 44 exact-determinant
   rows are not silently routed through an analytic method whose schedules do
   not cover them.
6. The active H8--H27 type-B frontier is now one 337-case bounded-Littlewood
   determinant/CRT certificate through moment 121.  It derives every bad
   count as `stable - finite`, proves a CRT modulus beyond the stable-moment
   uniqueness bound, and checks the half-stable margin directly.  The mixed
   H9--H22 programs run with `RUN_B=0`, retaining only their inexpensive
   type-C formulas; H23--H27 already had this mode.  The high-memory
   reverse-Pieri traversals remain optional independent controls.
7. The half-stable bridge no longer traverses both full diagonals at every
   Chain index.  For
   `A_n(k)=C(n,k-2)+C(n,k)-2C(n,k-1)`, its stable convolution has EGF
   `exp(-x+x^2/2)((1-x)^-3+(1-x)^-1)` and is generated by a fixed recurrence.
   The sign identity
   `sign(A_n(k))=sign((2k-n-2)^2-(n+2))` restricts negative terms to a
   central band of width `O(sqrt(n))`; positive active sums are recovered
   from the total by an at-most-436-term boundary subtraction.  A separable
   linear majorant is slightly more conservative than the former exact
   positive-part computation but certifies the same interval.  The final
   32-worker exact run took 119 seconds and about 1.5 GiB RSS, versus
   7,802--8,504 seconds in the archived full replays.  An exact startup test
   compares the recurrence and closed coefficient formula with the original
   binomial convolution through degree 128.

## Expensive Parts I--II stages

| Stage | Evidence of cost | Mathematical role | Cheaper sufficient route |
|---|---:|---|---|
| H8--H22 B reverse-Pieri frontiers | H21 alone is about 6,600 s on the documented host | Exact low-rank type-B bad-shape counts | Replaced by the unified 337-case determinant supplier; old traversal is optional. |
| H23--H27 B reverse-Pieri frontiers | Largest runs create 20,720,864 exact states; fleet notes report about 90 GiB aggregate memory | Same type-B correction inequality at later offsets | Replaced by the same determinant supplier; old shards are optional. |
| H28--H29 fleet | 25 historical heavy-partition tasks, including extra absorption stages | Overlap only | Removed from the mandatory dependency graph and clean-room replay. No replacement is needed. |
| Type-C frontier portions | Small FPF lists plus closed pause-polynomial and ratio checks | Type-C correction inequality | Retain the present formulas. They are already analytical; a character determinant would be less transparent and would not materially reduce cost. |
| Half-stable bridge scan | Archived runs took 7,802--8,504 s; the former code traversed `O(m)` large-integer pairs at each of 15,417 indices | Converts correction boxes to Chain lower bounds | Replaced by the EGF recurrence, central-band sign identity, boundary subtraction, and separable linear majorant above. The exact full run now takes about 119 s. |

The two largest identified Parts I--II bottlenecks have therefore been
removed: the mandatory replay contains one bounded-memory determinant
reconstruction in place of the high-memory H8--H27 type-B state maps, and
the bridge uses a proved EGF reduction instead of full quadratic
convolutions.  Both identities are proved directly in Parts I--II, so these
promotions do not introduce a circular dependence on Part III.

## Part III and other families

| Stage | Decision |
|---|---|
| B2--B21, C2--C28, D4--D70 finite hierarchy | Unified exact bounded-Littlewood supplier is sufficient. The old B/D reverse-Pieri and degree-40 reverse-Pieri/modular checks are optional overlaps. |
| High and middle B/C/D tails | Keep the outward-rounded MPFR certificates. These are already analytical determinant/Fredholm arguments and the audited runs are small. |
| Six polynomial and six rational-cap tails | Keep exact rational proofs. They run inside the determinant supplier and have negligible cost. |
| 58 directed low tails | Keep directed MPFR. Replacing these by exact symbolic inequalities would increase proof complexity; the current schedules are small and have positive audited margins. |
| Type A and exceptional prefixes | Keep exact moment suppliers. Their scopes are already truncated at the first proved analytic tail/bridge and are not responsible for the large replay cost. |

## Replay policy

The minimal theorem replay should run only load-bearing suppliers. Independent
algorithms remain important, but belong in
`full-q3-extension-independent-controls` or referee audits rather than as
duplicate prerequisites of the theorem build. A publication archive still
needs a clean, current-commit final replay; historical transcripts establish
overlap and provenance but do not substitute for that final-source run.
